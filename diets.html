Diets

Explore different diets based on your needs

Vegan

For those who prefer to not eat non animal products. A vegan diet is full of fruits and veggies with a reduced risk of heart disease. Many recommend taking B12 and iron supplements

View Related Recipes

Mediterranean

A lot of plant-based food similar to the vegan diet with the inclusion of dairy, fish and poultry. This diet promotes a reduced risk of cardiovascular disease and type 2 diabetes along with brain health and body weight.

View Related Recipes

Whole30

A 30-day reset diet that removes added sugar, grains, dairy, legumes, and processed foods. It helps identify food sensitivities and promotes whole, unprocessed eating. Ideal for short-term nutritional clarity.

View Related Recipes

Keto

The ketogenic diet is a low-carb, high-fat diet designed to put the body into a metabolic state called ketosis. It may promote weight loss and better blood sugar control, but requires careful planning to maintain nutrition balance.

View Related Recipes

Gluten-Free

Perfect for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. This diet eliminates all gluten-containing grains like wheat, barley, and rye, and focuses on naturally gluten-free foods like rice, vegetables, meat, and dairy.

View Related Recipes

Paleo

Vegetarian

A vegetarian diet excludes meat, poultry, and fish but often includes dairy products and eggs. It offers many health benefits, such as a reduced risk of heart disease and improved weight management, when carefully balanced.
